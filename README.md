## Introduction

Synthesizers are hypnotic. The beeps and boops they produce induce an almost involuntary urge to move. They enact on our primal need to respond to the call of rhythm and groove. For years I dreamt of the day that I would be the button pusher; the knob twister. The one who's delicate fingers' dance leads the listener on a journey of pure exaltation. Or something like that.

Anyway the point is I built an analog modular synthesizer. Its not really done, I left a lot of space for future additions with the intent to add a module to it every so often. Without further ado, here is the story of how I spent 2 years overcoming the many obstacles one faces when they decide to build an advanced electronics project with no previous experience and a lack of tools.

During my senior year of high school I decided to stop fucking around an challenge myself to build something truly *difficult*. I decided to pick a project described as follows on the documentation page: 

>This is an advanced project requiring understanding of electronics and electronic building and trouble shooting skills and equipment. Access to a good oscilloscope, DVM, bench power supply, soldering station and hand tools will be required. Only the schematic, circuit description, and suggested panel layout are provided. Please read over the entire project and make sure the information is clear enough for you to succeed before ordering PC boards or other products.
